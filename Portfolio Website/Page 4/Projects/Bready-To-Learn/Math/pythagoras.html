What is the Pythagorean Theorem?

The Pythagorean theorem is an equation that shows the relationship between the three legs of a right triangle.
The Pythagorean Theorem is a2 + b2 = c2

"a" is the length of one side next to the right angle.
"b" is the length of the other side next to the right angle.
"c" is the length of the hypotenuse, which is the side that is longest and opposite to the right angle.
